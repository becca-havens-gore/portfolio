Executive Summary

As Rapid7 scaled across multiple product lines, design quality diverged due to fragmented processes, inconsistent decision-making, and lack of shared governance. Designers were making major UX decisions in Slack threads, design drift increased release risk, and teams lacked visibility into standards or precedent.

I led the design and rollout of the Design Forum and the Rapid7 UX Standards Framework, establishing scalable governance, structured decision processes, and transparent alignment mechanisms. Together with UX leadership, engineering partners, and design system contributors, we created an operational foundation that improved accessibility, consistency, and cross-team cohesion across the entire design org.

The Problem

Rapid7's UX organization was growing rapidly across 6+ product teams, Platform UX, Design System team, Visual design, Documentation, and UX research. Yet the org lacked shared structures for evaluating patterns, approving new UI paradigms, logging decisions, ensuring cross-product consistency, and maintaining accessibility baselines.

Key Operational Gaps

  • No centralized review process: Important UX decisions were scattered across DMs and Slack threads
  • No governance model: Patterns were approved based on who reviewed them, not on a shared framework
  • No transparency: Designers lacked visibility into precedent or rationale for design decisions
  • No standards baseline: Accessibility, responsiveness, and usability varied widely
  • No systemic process for multi-team alignment: Cross-product flows suffered from inconsistent logic

The result: Rapid7's platform experience was becoming inconsistent, costly to maintain, and increasingly difficult to scale.

Research & Discovery

I conducted stakeholder interviews across design, engineering, product, and research. Clear patterns emerged:

Designer Feedback

  • "I'm not sure who approves this pattern."
  • "We reinvent the same components over and over."
  • "I want feedback earlier, not after something ships."

Engineering Partner Feedback

  • "UX asks for patterns we can't implement consistently."
  • "We need a single source of truth."

Leadership Feedback

  • "We need to reduce UX inconsistency risks."
  • "Standards need to be scalable, measurable, and enforceable."

Strategy & Architecture

The strategy centered around three pillars:

A. Structured Governance â€” Design Forum

I created the Design Forum, a recurring, formalized decision-making body for platform-level UX patterns.

Design Forum Components:

  • Recurring 30-minute sessions
  • Cross-disciplinary attendance
  • Standard submission template
  • Review process tied to the RDS lifecycle
  • Decision log stored in Confluence
  • #ux-all transparency updates
  • FOG model for evidence-based evaluation

FOG Model:

  • Facts: Research, data, accessibility requirements
  • Opinions: Design expertise, cross-team preferences
  • Guesses: Hypotheses needing testing

B. UX Standards Framework

I led creation of a four-pillar standard ensuring consistency across the platform:

  • Accessibility â€” WCAG 2.2 AA compliance
  • Responsiveness â€” Grid & breakpoint rules
  • Usability â€” Predictable interaction patterns
  • Governance & Continuous Improvement â€” Audits and lifecycle management

Each pillar included principles, requirements, success criteria, and measurement plans.

C. Operational Documentation

  • Decision logs
  • Pattern approvals
  • Exceptions tracking
  • Pattern lifecycle documentation
  • Rollout guidance

Implementation

Design Forum Launch

I facilitated cross-functional workshop sessions to align expectations, refine workflows, and practice using the FOG model on real design proposals.

Proposal Pipeline

I created templates used by all designers:

  • Problem statement
  • Goals
  • Supporting research
  • Alternatives considered
  • Accessibility considerations
  • Technical feasibility notes
  • Decision needed

Standards Integration with RDS

I partnered with the design system team to ensure:

  • Every RDS component adhered to the UX Standards
  • Every pattern proposal was reviewed through Design Forum
  • Every decision updated system documentation

Communication & Enablement

  • Weekly async summaries
  • Monthly highlights in #ux-all
  • Training for new designers
  • Accessibility onboarding
