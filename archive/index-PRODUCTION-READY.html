Philosophy

How I Build Cohesive Product Systems

01

Predictability over novelty

Interfaces should reward user intuition—not require memorization. Consistent patterns reduce cognitive load and build trust.

02

Accessibility as a constraint, not a compromise

If a component behaves beautifully with a keyboard, screen reader, and high-contrast themes—it behaves better for everyone.

03

Tokens before components

Good systems emerge from consistent primitives: spacing, radii, type scale, color, and motion. Components inherit clarity from their foundations.

04

Variants are earned, not accumulated

A new component variant must justify its existence through a true interaction need or semantic distinction—not designer preference.

05

Design debt prevented by automation

Linting, Figma tokens, automated audits, and version control keep systems healthy at scale—not heroics.

About Me

Designing systems that empower people

I'm a Design Systems Lead and Principal Product Designer with over 10 years of experience creating scalable, accessible design systems and enterprise UX frameworks. I specialize in unifying multi-product experiences, accelerating engineering velocity, and mentoring teams into design maturity.

My work spans four design systems across Rapid7, AlphaWave, and Kronologic, where I've led MUI migrations, built visualization frameworks, established governance models, and pioneered AI experience standards. I believe great design systems are more than component libraries — they're shared languages that unite design and engineering around a common vision.

When I'm not architecting systems, you'll find me mentoring designers, experimenting with AI-powered tools, or dreaming up backyard castle forts for my kids. I bring systems thinking, accessibility-first mindsets, and a deep commitment to craft into everything I do.
